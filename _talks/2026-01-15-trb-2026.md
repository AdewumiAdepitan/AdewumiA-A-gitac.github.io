I presented a poster at the **Transportation Research Board (TRB) 2026 Annual Meeting**. This page documents the poster, a brief summary of what I spoke about, and selected photos from the event.

## Poster Presentation
**Title:** *(detecting traffic safety risks using cloud-sourced near-miss data and Natural Language Processing)*

- Poster (PDF): [Download poster]({{ "/files/TRB2026/TRB2026_Poster_Adepitan.pdf" | relative_url }})

## What I spoke about
In this poster, I presented a data-driven framework for detecting traffic safety risks using **cloud-sourced near-miss data and Natural Language Processing (NLP)**, with a focus on pedestrian and cyclist safety. The motivation is that crash records are reactive and capture only a subset of hazardous events, while many risky situations occur repeatedly without resulting in reported crashes.

I explained how over **3,000 near-miss reports** from Northern Virginia Families for Safe Streets were processed using NLP to extract key risk behaviors from unstructured narratives, including **red-light running, crosswalk conflicts, and failure-to-yield**. These near-miss indicators were aggregated at the intersection level and linked with **more than 5,000 police-reported pedestrian and bicycle crashes** from VDOT (2017–2025).

Using a **Zero-Inflated Poisson (ZIP) model** accounting for roadway functional class, the results show that intersections with higher frequencies of specific near-miss behaviors—particularly red-light running—are significantly associated with higher crash occurrence. I concluded by highlighting how near-miss data can serve as an **early warning system** to proactively identify high-risk locations and prioritize targeted safety interventions before crashes accumulate.
